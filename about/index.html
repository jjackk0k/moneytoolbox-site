About

MoneyToolbox is a small, growing set of free personal-finance calculators. The whole idea is boring on purpose: type your numbers, get a straight answer, and see the formula that produced it.

Why it exists

Most "free" finance calculators online do three annoying things — hide the real answer under a pile of ads, gate it behind an email signup, or quietly send what you type to a server. This is the reaction to that. No signup. No ad walls over the result. No analytics tracking. The math is shown on every tool so you can check it against your own statement instead of trusting a black box.

How the numbers are verified

Every calculator's core math is covered by automated tests that run before anything goes live — for example, the debt planner is checked against hand-computed cases (a 0% loan must pay off in exactly the expected number of months; the avalanche method must never cost more interest than snowball). Tools also get re-checked on a regular schedule, and each page shows when it was last verified. If you ever find a number that looks wrong, please open an issue — that's the fastest way to get it fixed.

What this is, and isn't

These are calculators, not financial advice. They make simplifying assumptions (fixed rates, monthly compounding, no new charges or fees) that real-world accounts don't always follow, so treat the output as a clear estimate to reason with — not a guarantee, and not a substitute for talking to a qualified professional about your specific situation.
